Cold River Camp First 100 Years Celebration

 

In 2017 a Centennial Committee was formed by John Dean to begin planning activities for the 2019 celebration.  Members were Dwight Wilder until his untimely passing, Robert and Emma Crane, Amy Grover, Roberta Kaufmann and John Dean serving as chair. With regularly scheduled conference calls, the committee members began to plan for the Camp’s 100th birthday.

 

Our centennial celebration began on a brisk, sunny Saturday afternoon on May 11, 2019 during Opening Weekend when John Judge, Executive Director of the Appalachian Mountain Club joined us on the lawn near the Centennial Rocks. Following his remarks, during which he presented Roger Damon with the AMC Community Builder  award, we all enjoyed a piece CRC’s birthday cake.

 

We continued the celebration each week during the 2019 summer with evening programs illustrating the history of our beloved Camp, by encouraging hikers to follow the Bicknell Ridge Trail again, by selling special mementos and by releasing “Reflections - 100 Years of Friendship and Hiking at AMC Cold River Camp” - a 342 page book written by Robert and Emma Crane, long time volunteers at their beloved CRC.  Copies of the book may be purchased through the AMC’s Book Store or at CRC when it is open.
