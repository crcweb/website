Status Update (4/26/2019)

Hello AMC Cold River Camp Community,

Since our last update in December, the Camp has been under a deep fluffy blanket of deep white snow for most of the winter.  During this time, the Cold River Camp Lodge/Dining/Kitchen Planning Committee (Lodge Planning Committee or LPC) has been busy on an almost weekly basis.  Our Thursday evening conference calls now seem like a normal part of our schedule as we have actively discussed many alternatives and evaluated potential plans.  With the warmer weather on the horizon, we thought it appropriate to now sit down and provide the Camp Community with a broad update on some of the main points of our discussions. 

-The LPC wants to make sure that the Camp continues to prosper over the next century.

- The Lodge dining and kitchen are in need of attention to correct deficiencies

-There have been substantial alterations to Camp over the past century.

-Over the past four months the LPC has made a significant amount of progress in our planning effort

- The LPC discussed and evaluated the impact of minimum, intermediate, and expanded (or more extensive) levels of modification/renovation changes. 

-An initial study to look at the potential use of solar energy has begun

- The LPC is now at the point in the process where it would be helpful to get additional professional input on potential designs

- There are key factors that should be part of any future plans

- The LPC recently engaged a different architectural firm to develop a conceptual design.

- The LPC plans to review the outcome of this work during the next two months.

- The LPC encourages your feedback!  Any proposal from LPC will be reviewed with the CRC community for input before any final decisions are made.

First we want to provide you with our most recent collective perspective on the main reasons why we have embarked on this planning process.

Cold River Camp has operated successfully for one hundred years.  We want to make sure that the Camp continues to prosper over the next century.  This will require that we maintain an attractive and welcoming environment.  Making sure that our facilities are in good working order and provide the guests with highly functional common facilities, such as dining and social, will be important to our ability to continue to attract and keep future guests.

Lodge-related Camp facilities are in need of attention:

The dining porch roof is separating from the main roof. This part of the lodge sits on cinder blocks which are now settling.  The decay of this entire structure/addition to the lodge is accelerating and must be addressed soon. 

The current seating is perceived by many as crowded, especially in the dining porch.  In addition, it is difficult to see and hear the speakers during meal announcements.  We need to carefully consider ways of improving the dining area while maintaining the current intimate and unique dining experience.

We want to maintain an inclusive facility, which is consistent with AMC’s goals.  The camp is only ADA compliant in highly limited way.  This is particularly evident in the Lodge, one of the main common buildings at Camp, where there are major deficiencies.  For example, it is extremely difficult to accommodate more than one person with a wheel chair for a meal.  In addition, there is no ADA compliant bathroom in or near the Lodge. 

The kitchen is only compliant with building codes as required for the last time it was renovated, which is many decades ago.   A new renovation will allow us to bring the kitchen up to current code, improve the efficiency of its operation, and make it a safer and better working environment.

The social area of the Lodge seems small relative to the number of guests.  The social area becomes crowded at times, especially just before meals.  There are only about a dozen or so seats available for the roughly 70 guests when the Camp is full.

Addressing these concerns as a whole, before they become an issue will allow a comprehensive, holistic and well thought out solution, rather than the implementation of undesirable less optimal and/or piecemeal emergency measures hastily put in place to keep the camp operating.

Although you may think the mystique of the Camp is that the Camp never changes, there have been substantial alterations over the past century.  The present Camp Committee and those of the past worked hard to make sure that modifications of the facilities were done in a respectful way so as to fit in well and maintain the notion that “things stay the same at Cold River Camp”.  For example, do you know that there are three cabins that were built from the ground up during the past 10-15 years?  Two were replacements for existing cabins, and one is completely new (Notchview, Alice, and Caribou).  Although most cabins have a fireplace with a stone chimney, each of these three “new cabins” has a small wood stove and no stone chimney. 

Another example of dramatic change at Camp can be viewed through the Lodge.  Its three functional areas include social, dining, and food preparation (kitchen).   Each of these areas has changed dramatically over the past 100 years.  The original building did not have the dining porch or the current kitchen. The kitchen was behind the fireplace and the social and dining areas were in front of the fireplace.  All 37 guests for the first summer ate there (we now have over 550 during the summer).  Over time, a new building was added on to the Lodge to create a new kitchen and the area behind the fireplace became a dining area.  Eventually a dining porch that could hold four tables was added.  A major controversy occurred at Camp when a proposal was made to increase the dining porch area so that it could hold five tables in the 1980s.   The Camp is doing well today because of these modifications that blend in so well with our view of Cold River Camp.

Enough perspective.  What have we been working on? 

Over the past four months we have made significant progress in our planning.  We continued to systematically review and evaluate various designs, including those submitted through Community feedback.  We compared these designs with the current Lodge in four areas:  functional/operational; aesthetics/historical; climatic performance/comfort; and sustainability.   Although the group did not accept any of these designs, we did gain a great deal of knowledge by assessing the pluses and minuses of each proposal.  We will use this information as we move forward.   A more recent proposal referred to as the “East Expansion” did receive some positive responses.  This potential plan suggests moving all dining into the existing old kitchen space, which is probably at least 70-80 years old, and building a new kitchen to the north and east.  At this point this concept has not been fully vetted. 

We also evaluated on a broad-brush basis the impact of a minimum, intermediate, and expanded (or more extensive) potential Lodge changes.  The purpose of this exercise was to allow us to assess the potential impact of different levels of efforts on the Lodge dining, kitchen, and social areas.  The result of this exercise will then be applied as we go forward.  The Committee more recently took a step back and closely examined our project planning in terms of what we have done or need to do relative to a variety of factors such as project program; project criteria; existing conditions documentation; funding, financing, and budgeting; professional services; project delivery methods; and potential schedule.  This provides us with the areas where additional work will be required. 

We did an initial study to look at the potential use of solar energy in the Lodge project as a way to significantly reduce the Camp’s future carbon footprint.  Initial results were encouraging.   We are now in the process of doing an updated energy assessment of Camp that will aid us in determining our solar needs and options.

After much discussion, it was decided that we are now at the point in the process where it would be extremely helpful to get additional professional input on potential designs to evaluate going forward.  The Lodge Planning Committee has spent a great deal of time and effort evaluating the current Lodge and considering various ways to improve the dining, kitchen, and social areas. 

We have identified some of the key factors that should be seriously considered in any future plans.  These include: 

A: accommodating the needs of the summer season as a priority, but recognizing that there are other needs during Extension and the offseason that we should consider in our planning;

B: developing a single dining room at least in feel that will still offer an intimate dining experience with tables anchored to walls (similar feeling to our current dining area) with 12 6-person tables but large enough to add another table if a future CRC Committee decides there is a need;

C: increasing accessibility by making sure that the dining area has enough room for people to easily get in and out of their seats, and accommodating at least four wheel chair guests at the tables;

D: including two ADA compliant bathrooms in the Lodge complex, per code requirements;

E: updating and modernizing the kitchen, which has not been renovated in decades; including incorporation of a grill that can eliminate the outdoor temporary grill shed;

F: increasing the social area space while accommodating the lunch tables, morning coffee and afternoon drinks, which need temporary space during parts of the day;

G. making solar power part of the planning as a way to offset the carbon footprint of the Lodge.

We recently engaged an architectural firm, PWWG, based in Pittsburg, Pennsylvania to develop a conceptual design for us that will build upon the Lodge Planning Committee’s work.   This firm has had extensive experience in working on historical properties.  Jan Irvin, who is a long-time Cold River Camp guest and has been on the Lodge Planning Committee for some time, is also an architect-employee of PWWG.  Several employees of PWWG, including Jan, are working on this project.  Jan is serving as a liaison on this project, and has temporarily stepped off the Planning Committee during this time.  Jan is a critical link in this project since he has been involved in all of the important Planning Committee meetings, fully understands the sense of the Planning Committee, and the sensitivities of the Cold River Camp Community. 

We plan to review the outcome of this work during the next two months, and hope that this will allow the Planning Committee to eventually reach a consensus on a proposed plan.  We will then share that proposal with the Cold River Camp Community.   If time allows, presentations will be made each week this summer at camp explain the proposed plan and soliciting feedback. The plans will also be made available on line for feedback.

We encourage your feedback!  If you have any comments to share with the Lodge Planning Committee, please go to Conant Lodge Planning Projects page on the AMC Cold River Camp web page (https://amccoldrivercamp.org/Pages/lodgeProject.html)  and scroll down to find “Community Lodge Feedback form (in green) to send us your views.  Here is a direct link to the Feedback Form (https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSd_X8vQ_-SkAioea2WZ7JUMfWgdEmRcWkV6wPFl_nevf2xg6A/viewform  ) .  PLEASE NOTE THAT THE CURRENT BACKGROUND MATERIAL POSTED ON THE CRC WEBSITE HAS NOT BEEN UPDATED YET.  However, we do plan to post more current documents in a month or so, including the PWWG material, sometime after the Lodge Planning Committee has had adequate time to review and discuss it.  

We look forward to hearing from you.  And, stay tuned for our next update…

John Dean, Vice Chair CRC Committee and Ian Duncan, CRC Committee Member on behalf of the Lodge Planning Committee

Status Update (12/19/2018)

Hello Cold River Camp Community!

This is the beginning of what will become more regular updates on the Lodge Planning Project. Each week last summer the Lodge/Dining/Kitchen Planning Committee (the Lodge Planning Committee) presented a Lodge planning status update as a way of actively communicating with the CRC Community about the Lodge Planning project. We received significant Community feedback as a result of those meetings, as well as from members who directly provided comments via mail, email or online. We have carefully reviewed and discussed all of that feedback over the past few months and incorporated it into our planning process.

The Lodge/Dining/Kitchen Planning Committee (the Lodge Planning Committee) has been extremely active since last summer, but remains in the planning phase. No specific proposal has been reviewed and gained wide acceptance even within the Committee, whether it be one of the six concepts presented in the summer or anything else. The Committee has met regularly in weekly conference calls since September.

Since last summer, the Committee has done the following:

a. revised and updated a proposed priority of requirements to help guide the Committee’s design choices;

b. done a quantitative assessment of the six design concepts from the summer as a basis for further discussion of their relative strengths and weaknesses as a mutual learning exercise;

c. systematically reviewed and evaluated the current Lodge dining, social, and kitchen areas in terms of the existing design by focusing on four areas:

·       functional/operational;

·       aesthetics/historical;

·       climatic performance/comfort;

·       Sustainability.

And as part of that review captured an extensive amount of detail about the uses and measurements and characteristics of the current Lodge main areas—dining kitchen, social, porch, bathroom, and have become more educated on some of the building and health code implications of potential changes, and become more aware of possible ripple effects that involve, e.g., Lodge foundation, septic system or electrical service.

The purpose of this review was to aid in the future development and evaluation of designs.

We have systematically reviewed all of the guest feedback since the beginning of this project to make sure we fully understand the suggestions and concerns received so far. We have incorporated this feedback, including specific alternate design concept suggestions, into our discussions and evaluations. We plan to continue to solicit and evaluate future feedback as well.

Since May we have expanded the Committee to include Jan Irvin, who is a professional architect with significant experience in the rehabilitation of historic structures. Jan is a long-time Camp guest, and is a volunteer member of the Committee. In addition, Laurie Barr, former CRC Manager, is now a permanent member of the Committee, and has participated in numerous meetings, allowing the valuable perspective she brings to be more fully present.

We plan to follow-up this communication with periodic updates. The purpose of these ongoing updates is to provide a high level overview of what the Committee has considered and evaluated so that the you have a better understanding of how the planning process is progressing. We encourage you to provide feedback and ideas by either going directly to the Camp’s website (by clicking “About CRC” then “Lodge Planning Project” then look for the “Community Lodge feedback form near the bottom of the page) or by using this link (here).

We look forward to hearing more from you, and to providing you with future updates.

John Dean, Vice Chair CRC Committee and Ian Duncan, CRC Committee Member on behalf of the Lodge Planning Committee

Status Update (5/4/2018)

There are two active committees and an architect:

·       An overall Lodge / Dining Area Committee (Emma Crane, Evan Doucett, Nancy Hartle, Bobby Kaufman, Amy Grover, Frank Mastro, Gary Munson, Mark Winkler) which is to looking at overall Lodge needs including Dining; (until mid-February this was two separate committees, overall Lodge, and Dining Room, which agreed to merge given overlap and determining that the bulk of the attention will be on the dining area and related aspects).

·       A Kitchen Committee (Garry Crane, John Dean, Frank Mastro, Cheryl Poirier, Gail Roberts, Jim Weston) which is to propose a recommendation for a major kitchen renovation.

·       Architect Anne Whitney of Portsmouth NH, who has extensive experience in working on historic structures and lives and volunteers in a very history preservation-minded town and came highly recommended by one of our committee members based on personal experience.

The two committees and the architect are working closely together. Additionally, we have greatly benefitted from the participation of past camp manager and still current volunteer Bill Waste.

A first cross-committees + architect meeting was held November 30, 2017 to essentially level-set – share information about work done in the past, review a draft statement of needs and considerations, ask questions and share observations.

The Kitchen Committee has been at work since May 2017, initially looking at renovations within the existing kitchen & shed footprint, working with users of our kitchen and also with kitchen designers and equipment vendors. A kitchen renovation by itself will be expensive enough to require a capital campaign, which will require AMC approval.

The initial committees focus starting in November 2017 was to produce a "requirements" stable draft - needs, considerations, constraints that would guide the exploration and assessment of solutions. Based on that, the architect has proposed designs. A stable requirements draft was achieved in early February.

A cross-committees call was held on Feb. 22 with the architect to review an initial draft plan proposed by her, which has since been revised to incorporate various comments and suggestions. Other sketches have been provided by various committee members for consideration, both in terms of appeal and feasibility, representing design concepts that emphasize several other potential solution characteristics. At present, the committees are looking at these sketches and the architect designs to formulate more of a consensus on which requirements and design aspects are most important, since some significant tradeoffs may have to be made.

The high level plan and schedule is to

1)     Come up with a specific proposal on Lodge improvements by late spring 2018, all of which will fall under a proposed capital campaign

2)     Obtain AMC approval and plan the capital campaign in summer and fall of 2018

3)     Conduct the capital campaign in 2019, in conjunction with our Centennial Celebration

4)     Construction schedules TBD; we want to ensure we keep Camp in normal operation

Members of the CRC community can follow status at this web page and can provide feedback. In addition, you are invited to call in to a conference call reviewing project status scheduled for the evening of Thursday April 26 at 7:30 PM EST, to permit CRC community members to telephone in and ask questions and provide input. Further details (phone number and access code) for calling in will be provided via email to the CRC community email address list. We may hold more than one of these community-wide update sessions.

Project History

If you’re wondering whatever ultimately happened to the original Dining Room project the Camp Committee worked in the spring of 2017 and decided to proceed with in May and then was tabled on July 18, or what was supposed to happen next, that specific effort is superseded by this new overall Lodge planning effort. It should be observed that even at the time, the Camp Committee had other things in mind, especially a major kitchen renovation and the potential incorporation of an indoor, handicap-accessible bathroom. The Camp Committee’s approach was one of divide and conquer – while realizing these were all related and needed to be understood to some significant degree together, they didn’t all have to be worked in lock step. Additionally, the Camp Committee had the resources to sequentially plan out and set the dining room project in motion to be ready for 2018 and then involve the CRC community still with the possibility of making modifications. The opportunity for 2018 is now behind us, and we are now in the more normal and expected mode of engaging CRC community members before any seemingly final decisions are made.

For perhaps a decade or so, there has been Camp Committee discussion of a major renovation to the kitchen area, which would require a capital campaign to fund.  A committee was created in April 2017 to define a proposal for the specifics of a renovation, essentially keeping within the footprint of the existing kitchen/shed space. The members were and still are Jim Weston and John Dean co-chairs, Garry Crane, Frank Mastro, Cheryl Poirier and Gail Roberts. They worked over the summer and fall, making great strides in proposed layout, work flow and equipment. The original plan was to share that work with the CRC community when a specific proposal was ready, but that took a pause in order to sync up with the new lodge projects reorganization.
