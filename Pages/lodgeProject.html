Lodge Planning Project

Status update 11/20/2019

The Lodge Planning Committee this past summer proposed the ‘East’ design concept to the CRC Community through weekly in-Camp presentations and finally an open web presentation on Oct. 16. The presentation that provides details of the East design concept and tells the story of how the committee came to that proposal is here. There was generally widespread and enthusiastic support, and so planning is proceeding accordingly.   At present the focus is on exploring financial feasibility, since the bulk of the cost would need to come through donations. An official decision to proceed - with corresponding capital campaign - is yet to be made.

We are immensely grateful for the commitment and persistence of the Lodge Planning Committee to work through this challenging exercise over the course of the past two years, and for the patience and feedback from the CRC community on design options and needs.

The Lodge Planning Committee was started work in fall of 2017. (There were initially actually two committees, an Overall Lodge Committee, and a Lodge Kitchen Committee that itself began in the spring of 2017. They were eventually combined into one). The effort was motivated by two well understood needs – the dining porch area has significant structural problems and the kitchen could use major improvements.  The Lodge Planning Committee of volunteers from the CRC community looked at the question of lodge improvements holistically. If there are some major needs identified, is there a larger set of needs or desires to consider, and what are the key design criteria? 2018 was a state of flux – identification and exploration of many design options presented to the CRC community and then a methodical analysis. By spring of 2019 an ‘East’ design concept had emerged that was then further developed and vetted by an outside architect firm, PWWG of Pittsburgh PA. The committee members unanimously felt that the East concept well met all the primary needs, whereas any other option did not. And so it was proposed to the CRC community this past summer.  After carefully deriving a total cost estimate for building the East concept, working with VIS Construction Consultants, the Lodge Planning Committee concluded its work, and held it last official meeting on Oct. 17. Well done! Lodge Planning Committee members and other primary contributors are listed in the East design concept presentation.

We see the immediate next steps as

1)     Exploration of affordability – i.e., donations

2)     Identification of a good local contractor and architect for the detailed design and construction

3)     Further detailed work on the kitchen part of the East layout with an outside consultant

4)     Begin constitution  of  a Capital Campaign Committee

All of the above are in progress. (1) is especially critical to assess and be assured of before the CRC Committee can decide to officially move forward – i.e., seek AMC headquarters approval and conduct the actual capital campaign.
